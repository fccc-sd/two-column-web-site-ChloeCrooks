The Arctic Fox, also known as the white fox, polar fox, or snow fox, is a small species of fox native to the Arctic regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are very common in the Arctic Tundra Bioome. They are very well apated in the cold tempatures thanks to their thick fur which also helps them camouflage. Their body length may range from 18 to 27 in. with a rounded body shape to help let little body heat escape.

They prey on small creatures like lemmings, voles, ringed seal pups, seabirds, fish and waterfowl. They may be also eat carrion, seaweed, insects, berries and other small invertebrate. Natural predators to Arctic Foxes are Arctic Wolves, Polar Bears, Red Foxes, Wolverines, Grizzly Bears, as well as Golden Eagles.
